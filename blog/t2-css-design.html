 Margin, Border, and Padding
09.28.14

In CSS, these three things are pretty crucial to knowing how to layout yoursite. Unless you plan on having nothing touching each other, it would be wise to know the difference.

Essentially, these three are just layers with one going over the other like a hierchy of important. Padding gets the most, so it will always be on top. Border would second and lastly margin. A good example is shown here:



In laymen's terms:

  • Orange is padding
  • Blue is border
  • Green is margin
